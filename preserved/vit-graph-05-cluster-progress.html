vit — cluster_progress (per-tool)

How a muscle cluster gets a strength Δ: direct observations on the cluster's base_exercise, or observations on related exercises projected onto it through the relationships graph — direct wins.

Reading the graph. cluster_progress() (top) loads the taxonomy, then for every cluster that declares a base_exercise runs the dashed per-window panel twice — once for the older window, once for the newer. Inside the panel the pipeline forks: the direct path (top lane) fits curve() on the base's own near-failure observations and scales the winning anchor by its laterality factor; the projected path (bottom lane) walks inbound projecting edges, projects every source observation onto the base's 1RM, and takes the median. _window_rm1 resolves the fork: direct wins whenever the base has any qualifying observation — projected values only fill gaps, because projection coefficients are priors until refit (dashed amber callouts). The two window anchors difference into Δ, which lands on the per-cluster row alongside curve_diff's shape-mismatch flags and the pushed-set count.
