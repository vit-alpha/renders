vit — the vit summary volume line (output construction)

Graph 9 of the series. Traces one number — the 1.5w workout-units figure — from a workout file's sets to the printed **Volume:** line.

Reading the graph. One number, left to right: a workout record decomposes into sections → entries → sets; every set goes through set_load_points(), which dispatches to one of three load formulas; session_load() sums the raw points across the whole session and divides once by LOAD_NORMALISATION (5000 raw points = 1.0 wu); _round_volume() snaps to the index's half-step convention; and the result is the 1.5w in the printed **Volume:** line of the vit summary activity-log entry.

Side branches (lighter / dashed): the cardio path is its own module — cardio_load_points() converts duration to zone-weighted minutes via a four-tier fallback (recorded zone seconds → average/peak HR → estimated HR → the exercise's expected zone, else 0), scales by the exercise's form ratio and the cardio norm, and pre-multiplies by 5000 so the session-level divide lands back in wu. The category list after the | comes from a separate walk of the same entries: each exercise's movement_pattern mapped through _PATTERN_NAMES, deduped in first-occurrence order.
