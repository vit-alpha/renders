vit — the ± band in vit suggest (output construction)

Graph 8 of the series. Traces one number — the ± 2.1kg confidence band — from raw sets to the printed suggestion line.

Reading the graph. One number, left to right: raw sets pass the observed_points() filters over a 90-day window, curve() segments away observations on a different physical machine, and variance_kg() turns the surviving points into the ± band. The band is a heuristic, not a residual: 1.0 kg base, plus 0.6 kg per rep of extrapolation distance (target reps vs the nearest observed rep count), plus 0.25 kg per week since the most recent observation. It lands next to the kg figure on the printed line.

Side branches (lighter): the kg figure itself comes from the recency-decayed 1RM winner (prior chosen per exercise via curve_prior_for — epley by default, brzycki or lombardi when set) inverted at the target reps by estimate_weight_at(). Form-limited exercises (dashed) bypass the whole curve: the suggestion is the heaviest recent working set, variance is None, and the CLI prints the [form-limited] tag plus the exercise's form_progress_note cue instead of a band.
